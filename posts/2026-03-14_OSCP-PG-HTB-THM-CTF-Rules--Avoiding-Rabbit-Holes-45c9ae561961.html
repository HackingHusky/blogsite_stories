OSCP/PG/HTB/THM CTF Rules, Avoiding Rabbit Holes

I made myself a set of rules for every CTF I encounter. And honestly, it’s great to do some CTFs and pwn some boxes. However, it’s like a puzzle box, not an actual real-life engagement. Others have done the engagement and found some great CVEs, but each setup is like a Rubik's Cube on a computer. You’re caught in a web of what else to do or try, and test your problem-solving skills. Like the CPTS/OSCP/(dare I say) The PT1( which Try Hack Me, certs don’t land jobs. Stop over-promising. But the practice is so-so.)/eJPT/PJPT or PNPT (actually the greatest hands-on, actual, real-life attacks you would be doing daily.), You will be tested on problem-solving skills, especially when you're first starting or studying for an exam. These are some things I had to learn the hard way, and I really want everyone to achieve their goals. I would rather share what I learn than hide it.

These are tips to avoid some rabbit holes you can encounter while you try to work out the problem:

  1. Set a timer. Don’t burn yourself out trying to work on an issue you are not making progress on. Set it for 25 minutes, up to a maximum of 2 hours. With the intense test like the OSCP, you don’t have time to screw around with a port that’s not working. Take a step back. Give your brain a minute to think and come back to it when you feel ready. It’s not going anywhere. It's not worth crashing out or burning yourself out when you are stuck.
  2. Make each progress note, good or bad. Record the output of everything you’re testing. You need to get this skill down in real life, too. You are always one exploit away from gaining access or finding a vulnerability. Suppose you take some really good, solid notes. You’ll be ok.

4. The box is worked in a certain way. Think about how the author intended the box to be. So take that extra moment to scan. It could be that it's really a one-off port someone is trying to use, or that you're learning about it on your own.

5. If the box doesn’t have a custom password list/web directory list, it's probably going to be common. The web directory list from seclists will work. If it's a password, they all use rockyou.txt. Again, don’t spend more than 5–10 minutes trying to crack this thing.

6. For the OSCP/CPTS, passing the hash is quicker than the AD section or an AD box. Get local Admin, dump hashes with Impacket/Mimikatz, or get AS-REPRoasting/Kerberoast. If you get an NTLM hash, just pass it. Enumerate with the hash. That is just as good as a password.

7. Rely on your instincts for PrivEsc. Manual is quicker than an automatic script.

8. Remember to Google dork or “” as your OSINT commands. Again, you could be one search away from that exploit.

9. Things are going to be tough; CTFs are not meant to be real-life pentesting, it's supposed to be the dojo. The moment you feel like it's too tough, treat it like a video game. There was a section where you felt stuck and didn’t know what to do. That is exactly like that.

Do these steps, and you’ll kill it. Thanks for reading.
