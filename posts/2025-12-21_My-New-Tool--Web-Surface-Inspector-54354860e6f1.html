My New Tool, Web Surface Inspector

I wanted to build out a new tool for the cybersecurity challenge we are all facing: website security and AI security. It’s also a never-ending battle to keep up with new attack surfaces, AI prompt injection, etc. What started as a simple web scraping project grew to, oh, you know, that’s not a bad idea if I was also able to learn whether AI is being prompted during investigation, as well as what vulnerabilities I can view during a pentest or investigation.

It can be daunting at times, but what if you could automate that process? That is something I wanted to develop, like the ability to run recon during a pentest or with a vulnerability scanner that web scrapes, reports from the CVE, and also checks for AI prompt injections.

Thinking about it, I already know enough Python, and that would be the perfect language to use on Linux since it’s a quick tool that only does recon and also inspects the page. So I grabbed my morning coffee and went to town.

I started with the quick, ok, what was the response to get 200? Everything I learned from Boise State left my head for a second, and I went to W3 schools to remember and felt that bit of defeat: “How did I forget to write a function?” Somehow it came back to me once I grabbed my second cup. I've been working as a field tech and system admin, nose to the grindstone with Sec+, PJPT, basic help desk, and studying for the OSCP. However, one of my greatest passions/weaknesses is coding. I don’t use it every day. My degree is in Computer Science, and my minor is Cybersecurity. I feel in hard times, but landed my first IT job in 2021, and have been trying to keep up with it as much as I can for almost the last 5 years.
