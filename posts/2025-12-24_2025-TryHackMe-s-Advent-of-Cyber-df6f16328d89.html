This year, I started a lot of my blog posts and YouTube channel, The Husky Hacker. So, instead of the basic write-up, I wanted to do a YouTube series on the Advent of Cyber. Honestly, I had a blast making them. There were many differences between the Red and Blue teams, which led me to study more Purple team concepts and build tools to help companies identify compliance gaps. The biggest one right now is AI. However, I made an article going over that:

The challenges themselves:

YARA Rules is something I haven’t encountered before, and I didn’t want to make a video on something I am now learning about, how to use the tool. However, if you follow along with the video on the subject and watch the one with GingerHacker, who can explain it much better than I can, the room will be somewhat simple.

The other video I didn’t make was the AI Assistant, and here’s why:

You're asking what the red team, blue team, and software are, and you get an SQL injection from the red team, which you plug into the website on the page. This room was extremely self-explanatory. I did have to ask for that example a few times to get it, but on my first try, I got it right away. I felt like that wouldn’t be a lesson here other than prompt injection. The other lab on prompt injection, “Day 8, Prompt Injection-Sched-yule conflict,” was terrific.

So instead of the average write-up, I put them all in a video in a live time/write-up on my channel. And building that out was a blast this year.
