Service-Specific “Quick Hits”

Keep these high-probability vectors in mind:

  • MSSQL (Port 1433): Use impacket-mssqlclient. Check xp_cmdshell immediately if you get a login.
  • Redis (Port 6379): Check for unauthenticated access to write an SSH key to the /root/.ssh/authorized_keys file.
  • NFS (Port 2049): Look for no_root_squash. If present, you can mount the share, create a SUID binary as root on your Kali machine, and run it on the target to gain root access.

Create a “Golden Image” of your Kali VM with all the tools you need (Ligolo-ng, BloodHound, Seclists, etc.) and take a snapshot. If your VM crashes mid-exam, you can’t afford to waste an hour re-installing tools. If your machine is acting up, ask a protoctor to check on it. Also, check whether the machine you're working on has any issues.

Ensure your Ligolo-ng or Chisel workflow is muscle memory. You should be able to set up a tunnel and run nmap through it in under 2 minutes. The focus should be on the target pc’s. Make this step second nature.

Managing “The Wall”

Around the 12-hour mark, you will hit “The Wall” — where you can’t see the obvious.

  • Rule: If you are stuck for 60 minutes on one node, walk away from the computer for 15 minutes. Leave the room. Do not look at a screen. Drink water or eat something with protein. Your subconscious needs “diffuse mode” thinking to make connections that your focused brain is blocking. Most OSCP “Aha!” moments happen in the kitchen or the bathroom, not at the keyboard.
  • The Re-Check: When you return, re-read your nmap output from line 1. Most students fail because they ignore a "weird" port (like 8000, 8080, or 5000) that looks like a duplicate of port 80 but isn't.
  • Did I look at the source code? (Ctrl+U on every web page.
  • Did I check for common files? (/config.php, /.env, /.git, /.backup).
  • Did I try “admin/admin” or “guest/guest”?
  • Is there a version number I missed? (Check the headers with curl -I <IP>).

If it’s late and you’re looping, sleep for 2–3 hours.

  • The Math: 2 hours of sleep + 4 hours of high-performance hacking is infinitely better than 6 hours of “zombie hacking” where you make typos and miss obvious flags.

Remember, too: A small “win” (like getting a low-priv shell elsewhere) provides the dopamine hit needed to break the mental block on the harder machine.

It’s ok to use AI to help study. But don’t make it a habit. It’s ok to Google. It’s not going to stop in searches; don’t use the deep dive mode. Use guest or incognito mode to hide some features. Or use DuckDuckGo.
