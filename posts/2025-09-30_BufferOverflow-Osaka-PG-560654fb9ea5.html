This is a buffer overflow attack.

Make it executable and run:

chmod +x exploit.py

Successfully got a shell with a buffer overflow!!

It was interesting to say the least, because this was one area I wasn’t really focused on studying; however, as part of testing and things going wrong, the application was needed. Really, anything can happen during a real penetration test, not just on an exam. I can also see why it could be removed due to time constraints, but still, performing the attack is worth it because you may need to run this in the field.

The security concern is weak or anonymous access in general. However, if you are allowed to try it and it's on a test, and this approach works to establish a reverse shell, it's a free game. Nevertheless, don’t spend too much time on this if it doesn’t work—still a fun exercise for it.
